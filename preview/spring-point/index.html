Key terms

Policy Policy is a set of principles and statements that everyone must comply with. It provides the intent and scope of information security, but not the detail about how information security is implemented. Standards, Guidelines, Processes and Procedures support the Information Security Policy.
Principles Principles are a set of ethics/codes of practice from which the Information Security Policy can be derived. These are the basic information security principles that Latitude shall implement across the Organisation.
Standards Standards are implementation-level details that help to enforce and support the Information Security Policy.
Guidelines Recommended guidance to support Standards or serve as a reference when no applicable Standard is in place.
Processes and Procedures Processes and Procedures are step-by-step instructions to assist with the implementation of Policy, Standards and Guidelines.
Assets Physical information technology (hardware) and information-related assets.
Devices Physical information technology (hardware), such as mobile phones, laptop computers, tablet computers, removable storage media, that is provided to employees to perform their work-related activities.
Employees Latitude employees and contractors, and the personnel for suppliers who have access to Latitude devices, information, information systems and facilities.
Facilities Latitude offices, premises, sites, data centres that host information, information systems.
Information/Information Asset/Information System Information and information technology, including software, hardware and data.

Information Security: It’s everyone’s responsibility

Welcome to Latitude’s Information Security Acceptable Use course!

This Information Security module is designed to help you learn how to better protect yourself, your family, and Latitude. And today, this is more important than ever. Without the right actions in place, we are at risk of all kinds of information security threats - not just for ourselves, but for our communities too.

Understanding the latest threats can seem overwhelming, but this module simplifies the process by going over what threats are important to know about and why they’re important to understand.

Chapter 6

Browse Smart

The Missing Link to Safe Browsing

How can we check our links?

Ensuring that we check URL links before clicking and risking our information security is important. What are the different ways to reveal the true link?

Select all correct answer/s, then select Submit.

That’s right.

Actually…

There are some more ways you can check the URL link before clicking it. You can hover over the link, hold down on the link on your mobile, or check the website link at the bottom of your device screen after scanning the QR code.

Link safety

When checking if a link is safe, you can start by reading the URL. Remember to hover over the link to review it, before you click.

  • A URL starts with the protocol, http or https. When entering sensitive information, make sure it has the ‘s’ at the end as it’s more secure.
  • A subdomain is used to create a separate section of the main website. It’s common for attackers to use company names as the subdomain, to trick people into believing it’s a legitimate website i.e http://lattitude.loginnow.com.
  • A domain name normally consists of the company name. Make sure this is spelled correctly.
  • A top-level domain (TLD) comes after the final dot in the website’s domain name. It’s common for attackers to use to different TLD to create a look-alike URL to the genuine website.
  • Finally, the path is everything after the ‘/’. It indicates the exact location of a page.

Hover before you click.

Using Generative AI at Latitude

At Latitude, we support the use of generative AI. However, we all need to remember not to type sensitive or confidential information into it. This helps us keep our information secure and that of those around us safe.
